Metric Stress

The stress of a projection measures the discrepancy between distances in the high-dimensional (i.e., geospatial) and the low-dimensional (i.e., projected) domain. Stress values are scaled between 0 and 1, where 0 means no stress, which is desirable. This is calculated as the normalized sum of squared residuals between the pairwise distances in the original and projected space.

Galbraith, J. I., Moustaki, I., Bartholomew, D.J., and Steele, F. The Analysis and Interpretation of Multivariate Data for Social Scientists. CRC Press, Feb. 2002. ISBN: 978-1-58488-295-4.

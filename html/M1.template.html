M1: Neighborhood Trustworthiness

This metric signifies the number of data points in the low-dimensional (i.e., projected timeline) k-neighborhood that were not members of the high-dimensional (i.e., geospatial) neighborhood. The k is chosen based on the data, and the high-dimensional distances are ranked (i.e., nonmetric). The measure is normalized to the interval [0, 1], where a value of 1 means perfect preservation of neighborhood.

Venna, J., & Kaski, S. (2001, August). Neighborhood preservation in nonlinear projection methods: An experimental study. In International Conference on Artificial Neural Networks (pp. 485-491). Springer, Berlin, Heidelberg.
